POAP - The Proof of Attendance Protocol

Go places. Get badges. Dazzle your friends.

POAP is a protocol for issuing and displaying cool crypto-badges originally built in the EthDenver 2019 hackathon.

FAQ

What is POAP again?

It's a system that event organizers can easily use to distribute attendance crypto-badges to people that show up, and a tool for attendees to display and share the badges they have obtained.

Why would I want to get badges?

Here are some things that could happen when you obtain POAP badges:

  • Collect cool badges
  • Impress your crypto buddies(*)
  • Enter a Lambo giveaway(*)
* Results not guaranteed. Any expectations of recognition, prizes, privileges or earnings are your own.

How are POAP badges implemented?

POAP badges are ERC-721 tokens. Once minted, they can be viewed on POAPscan or any NFT-enabled interface (Etherscan, Rarebits, OpenSea).

How do I get badges?

How you get badges depends on the alternative(s) chosen by the event organizer. For our ETHDenver launch, we are distributing badges with an open WiFi hotspot at our table (POAPFi) that gives you access to http://poap.fi/ethdenver, or manually (find us and we'll give you a badge!).

How can I use POAP for my event?

If you want to use POAP to distribute attendance badges at your event, contact us so we can hook you up. All you need to provide is a badge design and some event metadata (name, location, date).

Who is using POAP?

All the cool kids are doing it.

What are some things that could be built on POAP?

Here are some truly marvelous features and ideas that this hackathon was too brief to contain:

  • Integrate a proper proof of space-time protocol (XYO, FOAM) for more resilient presence attestation
  • Integrate with Kickback to seamlessly handle refunds of deposits to people that attend (send deposit, attend event, get badge + automated refund)
  • Implement privacy preserving mechanisms (proxy reencryption?) for finer grained disclosure of information (i.e. prove you went somewhere without disclosing your entire list of past events)
  • Use badge ownership as a soft reputation layer for streamlined access to events or meetups
